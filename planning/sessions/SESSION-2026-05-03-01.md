## Appendix D — Three-explicit-pause-points-at-irreversible-moments candidate-pattern (Day 12 A3.7)

**First occurrence; awaits second-occurrence-for-ratification per discipline-maturation pattern.**

### What it is

A refinement of the "report between steps; proceed unless intervened" discipline with explicit operator-go-confirmation gates at irreversible-or-near-irreversible moments. The discipline as written assumes auto-progression unless operator intervenes; the refinement carves out specific pivot points where auto-progression would defeat the check's purpose.

### Surface

A3.7 ratification: "Three pause gates at the pivot points where auto-progression past a check would defeat the check's purpose." Concretely:

- **Pause 1 (A3.1 pre-push enumeration → step 3 remote-add):** if the enumeration surfaces unexpected files or surprising count, the right behavior is to not proceed until investigated. Auto-progression would land remote-add on top of a check meant to surface concerns.
- **Pause 2 (step 3 remote-add → step 5 push-command-surface):** if the `git remote -v` output shows unexpected URL or auth state, don't proceed until investigated.
- **Pause 3 (step 5 push-command-surface → operator-runs-push):** the publish keystroke is operator-side per A3.2; structurally enforced by channel-purity decision.

### Why it matters

The "report between steps; proceed unless intervened" discipline works for reversible work (incremental commits; docs updates; test additions). For irreversible-or-near-irreversible operations (public push; force-push; package deletion; production deployment), the same discipline produces wrong behavior — auto-progression past a check defeats the check's purpose. The refinement: at irreversible moments, switch to explicit-go-confirmation mode where Claude Code surfaces and waits.

### Practice

When approaching an irreversible-or-near-irreversible moment, surface explicit-pause-point framing in the task surface ("step 4 is operator-confirmation gate before step 5"). After Claude Code completes pre-pause work and surfaces results, do NOT proceed until operator explicitly confirms go. The pause is the discipline.

### Worked example

A3 push sequence: seven steps total; three explicit-pause-points at steps 2 (post pre-push enumeration), 4 (post remote-config sanity), 6 (operator runs push). Auto-progression past any of these would have landed the push without the corresponding check serving its purpose. The pause caught zero issues this session — but that's the design: the pause exists for the failure mode, not the success mode.

### Forward-carry

Awaits second-occurrence (any future irreversible-or-near-irreversible operation: force-push, package-deletion, production-deployment, branch-deletion, history-rewrite, etc.) for ratification eligibility.
